# Neural Inertial Odometry and PDR for a Route-Constrained iPhone Prototype — Research Report

Date: 2026-06-10 · Produced by deep-research agent (web sources cited inline)

## 1. Neural inertial odometry: state of the art

**The core idea.** Instead of double-integrating acceleration (drifts in seconds) or counting steps, neural inertial odometry (NIO) regresses velocity/displacement from short IMU windows. IONet (2018) and RoNIN established that this beats classical PDR by large margins ([RoNIN paper](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/1905.12853), [survey: Deep Learning for Inertial Positioning](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2303.03757)).

**RoNIN (ICRA 2020)** is still the reference baseline. Dataset: 42.7 h of IMU + ground-truth motion from 100 subjects, 3 Android phones, with *natural* handling — in hand, deep in pocket, in bag ([project site](https://ronin.cs.sfu.ca/), [paper](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/1905.12853)). Method: a 1D ResNet-18 regresses 2D velocity in a "heading-agnostic coordinate frame" (any gravity-aligned frame), from 1 s windows of 200 Hz IMU, using the OS rotation vector for the body→gravity-frame transform. Numbers (from the paper): on unseen subjects, ATE 5.14 m / RTE 4.37 m (RTE = drift per 1 minute) for ResNet, vs **ATE ~28 m / RTE ~23 m for a classical step-counting PDR baseline** — roughly a 5x improvement, and the model stays usable across hand/pocket/bag because those poses are in training data. So the realistic expectation for a well-trained RoNIN-class model on a phone is **~3–5 m of drift per minute of walking**, heading drift being the dominant term.

**TLIO (RA-L 2020)** wraps a displacement+uncertainty ResNet in a stochastic-cloning EKF that also estimates IMU biases; it outperforms RoNIN-3D in position and an AHRS in orientation ([project page](https://cathias.github.io/TLIO/), [paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2007.01867)). The survey reports both IONet and TLIO keep error **within ~3 m 90% of the time over 3–7 min sequences** ([survey](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2303.03757)). Caveat: TLIO was trained on 60 h of *headset*-mounted data; out-of-distribution motions (side-stepping, rolling chairs) are documented failure cases, and there's no published phone-domain validation — expect degradation on handheld/pocket iPhone without retraining.

**NILoc (CVPR 2022)** does absolute *relocalization* from inertial velocity sequences via a transformer, but requires building-specific training on ~tens of hours of data per venue (53 h dataset) ([NILoc](https://sachini.github.io/niloc), [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.15851)) — not practical for your use case, though conceptually it validates "motion sequence → location on known map," which is exactly what your route matcher approximates cheaply.

**RIO (CVPR 2022)** introduces rotation-equivariance as a *self-supervised* signal: a model trained with it on **30% of the data matches the full-data model**, and adaptive test-time training improves generalization on unlabeled data ([RIO paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.11676), [CVPR PDF](https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2022/papers/Cao_RIO_Rotation-Equivariance_Supervised_Learning_of_Robust_Inertial_Odometry_CVPR_2022_paper.pdf)). This matters for you: it directly reduces how much labeled data adaptation needs.

**2023–2026 wave** (mostly architecture refinements on the RoNIN/TLIO recipe): EqNIO (ICLR 2025) learns an equivariant gravity-aligned frame and plugs into both TLIO and RoNIN pipelines, improving generalization on TLIO/Aria/RIDI/OxIOD ([EqNIO](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.06321), [code](https://github.com/RoyinaJayanth/EqNIO)); IONext (2025) is a pure-CNN model (11 M params, 73 MFLOPs) claiming best ATE on RoNIN/RIDI/RNIN benchmarks, ~45% better than the transformer iMOT ([IONext](https://arxiv.org/html/2507.17089v1)); also RIOT (transformer, 2023) ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2303.01641)), X-IONet (2025) ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.08277)), Lie-event NIO (2025) ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/html/2505.09780v1)), GNIO (gated TLIO, 2026) ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.15281)), and IMUNet (mobile-efficient, IEEE TIM 2024). None of these change the deployment picture fundamentally; gains over RoNIN-ResNet are incremental (tens of percent, not orders of magnitude).

**TinyOdom (IMWUT 2022)** is the most deployment-relevant: NAS-optimized TCNs of **27–72 kB** achieve 2.5–12 m error over 60 s on microcontrollers — proof that NIO does not need a big model, and that "very small amounts of data in a new domain can fine-tune a pretrained model to work reasonably in the real world" ([TinyOdom](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10957141/), [code](https://github.com/nesl/tinyodom)).

## 2. Practical iOS deployment

**Weights and licenses.** RoNIN code is GPL-3.0, but the **dataset and pretrained models are under a custom non-commercial/research-only license** ([GitHub](https://github.com/Sachini/ronin), [FRDR record](https://www.frdr-dfdr.ca/repo/dataset/816d1e8c-1fc3-47ff-b8ea-a36ff51d682a)) — fine for prototyping, not shippable in a commercial Headout product without retraining on your own/licensed data. TLIO's repo expects you to retrain (its dataset is CC-BY-NC; models ship as TorchScript) ([TLIO GitHub](https://github.com/CathIAS/TLIO)). EqNIO and TinyOdom publish code openly. **No published, documented iOS deployment of RoNIN/TLIO-class models was found** — research apps and data-collection rigs are Android-based; on iOS the closest public artifacts are ARKit-based loggers used for ground truth ([ARKit-Data-Logger](https://github.com/PyojinKim/ARKit-Data-Logger)). You'd be doing first-of-its-kind-in-public integration, but nothing about it is exotic.

**Core ML conversion.** RoNIN-ResNet is a vanilla 1D ResNet-18 (~4.6 M params, ~10–20 MB at fp16/fp32); Conv1d/LSTM/TCN ops all convert cleanly with [coremltools](https://github.com/apple/coremltools) from PyTorch. TLIO is harder: the network converts, but the stochastic-cloning EKF is host code you'd port to Swift yourself (it's a few hundred lines of filter math; the repo's Python EKF is the reference).

**IMU rates and preprocessing.** This is the main gotcha: RoNIN expects **200 Hz** IMU; iPhone's `CMDeviceMotion` tops out at **100 Hz** ([NSHipster/CMDeviceMotion](https://nshipster.com/cmdevicemotion/), [Apple forums](https://developer.apple.com/forums/thread/67639), [WWDC23 Core Motion session](https://developer.apple.com/videos/play/wwdc2023/10179/) — the new 800 Hz batched APIs are Apple Watch-only). Options: linearly upsample 100→200 Hz (works in practice, slight distribution shift), or retrain at 100 Hz (cleaner). Preprocessing is otherwise a great fit for iOS: RoNIN's heading-agnostic gravity-aligned frame is exactly what `CMDeviceMotion.attitude` with `.xArbitraryZVertical` gives you (the iOS analog of Android's game rotation vector RoNIN uses); rotate device-frame accel+gyro into that frame, feed 1–2 s windows.

**Inference cadence and battery.** Velocity outputs are integrated at ~1–20 Hz; one ResNet-18-1D forward pass per second is negligible on the Neural Engine (sub-millisecond-class; IONext-scale models are 0.07 GFLOPs ([IONext](https://arxiv.org/html/2507.17089v1)) vs. multi-GFLOP vision models phones run at 30 fps). The real battery cost is keeping the app foregrounded streaming 100 Hz device motion — which you already pay today. By contrast, `CMPedometer` runs on the always-on motion coprocessor at effectively zero app-side battery cost ([Apple motion coprocessors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_motion_coprocessors)).

## 3. Fine-tuning and using your survey data

Evidence says adaptation is cheap relative to training from scratch: TinyOdom fine-tunes pretrained models into new domains with small data ([TinyOdom](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10957141/)); RIO matches full-data performance with 30% data and supports test-time training on *unlabeled* data via rotation-equivariance ([RIO](https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.11676)); MotionTransformer adapts across placements without new-domain labels ([MotionTransformer](https://www.cs.ox.ac.uk/files/10408/motiontransformer-transferring-neural%20(3).pdf)); the survey covers further transfer approaches ([survey](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2303.03757)). Order of magnitude: think **a few hours of trajectory-labeled walking on your target device** to fine-tune RoNIN-class models, not 40 h.

But: survey passes **as currently recorded are weak labels** — checkpoint timestamps give per-segment average velocity, not the per-second velocity vectors RoNIN-style supervision wants. They're usable for (a) calibrating step length per segment, (b) validating any model, (c) weakly supervising total-displacement losses — but not for proper fine-tuning. The fix is free: **run ARKit world tracking during survey passes** and log its 6-DoF pose at 60 Hz alongside the 100 Hz IMU. ARKit VIO is exactly how the RIDI/RoNIN-era datasets got ground truth (Tango/ARKit-class VIO; see [ARKit-Data-Logger](https://github.com/PyojinKim/ARKit-Data-Logger)); surveyors hold the phone up anyway. That single change makes every future survey pass a training/fine-tuning sequence.

## 4. Cheaper wins below full neural odometry

**CMPedometer first.** Clinical validation: iPhone step counting is highly concordant with ActiGraph (bias −7.2% ± 13.8% even in impaired-gait patients), while its *distance* estimate overestimated by 43% ± 42% due to uncalibrated stride length — correctable to 8% ± 32% with a per-user factor ([npj Digital Medicine validation](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41746-018-0073-x), [MobiHealthNews summary](https://www.mobihealthnews.com/news/study-iphones-step-tracker-solid-dont-rely-its-distance-measurement-features), [MDPI criterion-validity study](https://www.mdpi.com/2227-7080/9/3/55)). Apple's detector is pose-robust (pocket/bag) because it's tuned on the motion coprocessor across carry modes — almost certainly more robust than a hand-rolled peak detector in untested pocket/bag carry, and free. Use `CMPedometer.startUpdates` as a second step source and reconcile; also use its `distance` with per-segment calibration rather than its default stride.

**Pose-robust classical step detection.** If keeping the custom detector: adaptive peak–valley magnitude *and* temporal thresholds derived from running statistics reach ~98.6% accuracy across walking/running × hand/pocket/bag/swinging ([MDPI Sensors 2015](https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/15/10/27230)); gyro energy is the more robust signal in swinging-hand carry ([motion-mode recognition](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3649428/)). Detecting carry mode and switching thresholds is the standard recipe.

**Step-length estimation.** Weinberg's quartic-root of (a_max − a_min) per step is the classical baseline; speed-adaptive variants cut error to a few percent across slow/normal/fast walking ([MDPI Sensors 2016](https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/16/9/1423)); learned regressors (StepNet) reach 2.1–3.2% error ([survey](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2303.03757)). The highest-leverage version is trivial: per-segment surveyed distance ÷ surveyed steps gives calibrated stride per segment, and a Weinberg-style amplitude term can modulate it within a pass to absorb speed changes.

**Turn detection as a route-consistency signal.** This addresses the worst failure mode (route-blind step accumulation). Project gyro into the gravity frame, integrate yaw rate; declare a turn when |Δheading| exceeds ~30–45° within a short window. Literature: gyro signatures at corners are reliable landmarks and improve heading along straights ([PDR + map matching](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/321262938_An_indoor_positioning_system_using_pedestrian_dead_reckoning_with_WiFi_and_map-matching_aided)); UnLoc used turns/elevators as organic landmarks to reset dead reckoning, achieving **1.69 m median error** ([UnLoc, MobiSys 2012](https://synrg.csl.illinois.edu/papers/unloc.pdf)); sequences of (orientation-change, segment-length) pairs can be matched to a map with an HMM, insensitive to initial state ([IEEE TIM 2023](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10130102/)); trajectory-vs-map geometric similarity corrects gyro drift ([PDR + magnetic matching](https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/18/12/4142)). For a known route this collapses to something very simple: precompute the route's turn signature (signed heading change at each cumulative distance from survey passes — the gyro logs already contain this); at runtime, align the detected turn sequence to it (greedy or DTW). Each matched turn is a hard progress anchor that *snaps* the estimate and zeroes accumulated step error; an expected-but-missing or unexpected turn is an off-route/pacing-in-place flag. This is the published mechanism (landmark resets + HMM sequence matching) applied to a 1D route — the easy case.

## 5. Apply to our prototype — prioritized

### (a) Quickest classical improvements (days each)
1. **Record more in surveys now** — see (c); do this before anything else so data accrues.
2. **Turn anchors** (~2–4 days): extract route turn signature from existing survey gyro logs; runtime turn detector (gravity-frame yaw integration); snap progress at matched turns; flag missing/unexpected turns as route-consistency failures. Biggest accuracy win per effort; directly fixes route-blindness ([UnLoc](https://synrg.csl.illinois.edu/papers/unloc.pdf), [HMM matching](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10130102/)).
3. **CMPedometer as parallel step/distance source** (~1 day): coprocessor-grade pose robustness for free; reconcile with the custom detector; keep per-segment distance calibration ([validation](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41746-018-0073-x)).
4. **Adaptive thresholds + carry-mode awareness** in the detector (~2 days): MAD threshold is good; add temporal-threshold adaptation and a gyro-energy channel for swinging/pocket ([MDPI 2015](https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/15/10/27230)).
5. **Weinberg-modulated calibrated stride** (~1 day): per-segment stride from surveys × amplitude factor; consider replacing the fixed 0.75/0.25 step/time blend with distance-based progress + turn snapping ([MDPI 2016](https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/16/9/1423)).

### (b) Realistic neural-odometry path for iOS
- **Phase 1 (~1–2 weeks):** download RoNIN-ResNet pretrained weights (research use OK), convert via coremltools, build the 100 Hz `CMDeviceMotion` → gravity-frame → upsample-to-200 Hz → 1 s-window pipeline, integrate velocity output, and replay against recorded Meadows walks offline. Expect ~3–5 m/min drift ([RoNIN](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/1905.12853)); along a constrained route with turn anchors that's plenty — velocity (not steps) becomes the progress rate and pacing-in-place reads as near-zero displacement.
- **Phase 2 (~2–4 weeks):** fuse RoNIN velocity into the route matcher (1D projection along route + turn anchors); evaluate vs step-based progress. Skip full TLIO/EKF unless heading drift proves limiting; if shipping commercially, retrain/fine-tune on own ARKit-labeled data (license constraint) — RIO-style equivariance augmentation cuts the data needed ([RIO](https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.11676)).
- Don't bother with NILoc-style relocalization (building-specific training, tens of hours — [NILoc](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.15851)) or chasing 2025 architectures yet; gains are incremental over RoNIN ([IONext](https://arxiv.org/html/2507.17089v1)).

### (c) Record NOW in surveys (so data supports training later)
1. **Raw 100 Hz accelerometer + gyroscope + `CMDeviceMotion` attitude quaternion, gravity, userAcceleration, magnetometer** — full streams with timestamps, not derived features.
2. **ARKit 6-DoF pose at 60 Hz** during survey passes (world-tracking session running alongside) — converts surveys into trajectory-labeled training data, the single most valuable addition ([ARKit-Data-Logger pattern](https://github.com/PyojinKim/ARKit-Data-Logger)).
3. **CMPedometer events** (steps, distance, pace) for cross-calibration.
4. **Carry-mode annotation** per pass (hand/pocket/bag) and **device model/iOS version** — placement is the main generalization axis ([RoNIN](https://ronin.cs.sfu.ca/), [MotionTransformer](https://www.cs.ox.ac.uk/files/10408/motiontransformer-transferring-neural%20(3).pdf)).
5. **Deliberate negative passes:** pacing in place, standing, walking off-route, reverse direction — needed to evaluate route-consistency rejection, which no public dataset gives you.

**Research caveats:** TLIO/headset-domain numbers may not transfer to handheld phones (no published phone validation); "3 m 90% of the time" figures come from each paper's own dataset/device distribution; RoNIN-license terms should be read in full before any commercial build; battery claims for 100 Hz streaming are inferred from platform documentation rather than measured for the app.
