# Heuristic & Opportunistic Augmentations for Phone-Only Indoor Dead Reckoning — Research Report

Date: 2026-06-10 · Produced by deep-research agent (web sources cited inline)

## 1. GPS / Core Location as an indoor anchor

The literature is unambiguous that sporadic indoor GNSS is a real, exploitable signal rather than pure noise. The most directly relevant work is **Zhou & Maekawa, "GPS-assisted Indoor Pedestrian Dead Reckoning" (IMWUT 2022)** ([dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3569467](https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3569467)), which proposes exactly the pattern we want: treat *window-side areas* as a new class of indoor landmark — the phone receives signals through windows from satellites at particular orientations, and these reception events correct accumulated PDR error **with no installed hardware and no fingerprint survey**. The key insight is that you don't need a usable lat-long fix; the *pattern of which satellites are receivable* is itself location-dependent and repeatable. For the route-constrained case, even cruder use works: a raw fix with 30–70 m uncertainty near an atrium still kills gross hypotheses ("user is in wing A, not wing C").

Quantitatively, deep-indoor smartphone GNSS is poor but bounded: studies report ~11.5 m CEP50 indoors in favorable buildings, ~7 m RMS for a Galaxy S20 in an instrumented deep-indoor setup, and multipath errors of tens of meters in hard cases ([GPS World](https://www.gpsworld.com/indoor-gnss-never-looked-better/), [ION Navigation](https://navi.ion.org/content/68/3/601), [PMC9788430](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9788430/)). On iOS specifically, `horizontalAccuracy` is an uncertainty *radius estimate*, not a guarantee ([Apple docs](https://developer.apple.com/documentation/corelocation/cllocationaccuracy)); indoors Core Location silently falls back to Apple's Wi-Fi positioning database, where median errors of ~74 m have been measured ([GLOBE study](https://www.globe.gov/documents/2631933/6965868/42534991.pdf)). Practical rule from the Freeletics GPS-testing writeup: gate on reported accuracy and treat anything >~30–50 m as a weak prior only ([freeletics.engineering](https://freeletics.engineering/2019/06/03/ios_gps_testing.html)). The right fusion is a **soft Bayesian observation**: weight each fix by `1/horizontalAccuracy²` and clamp the along-route position estimate to the fix's uncertainty disc intersected with the route polyline.

**Route initialization at the entrance**: building entrances are repeatedly cited as the canonical "precise GPS position and temporary starting point" for indoor PDR ([Map Aided PDR](https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=36346)). iOS region monitoring (geofencing) works for this but is coarse: Apple recommends ≥100 m radius, crossing notification requires ~20 s on the far side of the boundary, and latency runs seconds to minutes ([Apple forums](https://developer.apple.com/forums/thread/94091), [Medium deep-dive](https://dwirandyh.medium.com/deep-dive-into-core-location-in-ios-geofencing-region-monitoring-7846802c968e)). So geofencing is good for "user has arrived at the venue, start the engine and prefetch the profile," not for precise anchoring. `CLVisit` (visit detection) is even coarser — arrival/departure times accurate to a minute or two, poor `horizontalAccuracy`, undocumented dwell thresholds ([felginep.github.io](https://felginep.github.io/2020-06-09/visit-monitoring), [Apple CLVisit docs](https://developer.apple.com/documentation/corelocation/clvisit)) — useful only for analytics, not live anchoring. The better entry anchor: run `CLLocationManager` at best accuracy as the user approaches and take the **last good outdoor fix before signal degrades** as the PDR origin, which is what indoor-outdoor transition systems do ([Racko et al., door-crossing paper, arXiv:2209.11851](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2209.11851)).

## 2. Dead reckoning against a geo-referenced route polyline

This is mature technology from vehicle navigation transplanted to pedestrians. Map matching with a particle filter over a constrained path is the standard formulation: each particle carries position along the geometry; impossible particles (off-route, through walls) are killed; heading + step events propagate; occasional absolute observations re-weight ([Particle Filters for Positioning, Navigation and Tracking](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/3318231_Particle_Filters_for_Positioning_Navigation_and_Tracking), [hybrid PDR+map-matching system, PMC4721747](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4721747/)). The crucial empirical datapoint for semi-outdoor venues: magnetometer-aided PDR **without GPS** on a two-mile mountain trail achieved ~20 m average / <45 m max error, ~1.4% of distance traveled, by exploiting the path constraint ([same survey](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/3318231_Particle_Filters_for_Positioning_Navigation_and_Tracking)). A known route reduces the problem to **1-D (arc-length along the polyline)**, which is dramatically easier than 2-D: heading only needs to disambiguate direction of travel and detect route turns, and a GPS fix only needs to constrain progress, not position. Low-complexity online PDR correction using map matching + GPS jointly is published and works in the indoor-outdoor seam ([Taylor & Francis](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/10095020.2019.1617528)). Commercial proof for the semi-outdoor case: VoiceMap runs purely GPS-triggered route audio with a default 21 m trigger radius (minimum 15 m, below which "GPS bounce" causes failures) and explicitly warns triggers fail indoors and in covered walkways ([VoiceMap docs](https://docs.voicemap.me/tour-publishers/choosing-locations/)). For gardens/castles/open-air museums, an outdoor-grade map-matched GPS+PDR mode is essentially solved tech; the magnetic matcher is only needed in the roofed segments.

## 3. Magnetic/structural landmark detection as discrete anchors

The canonical lineage is **UnLoc (MobiSys 2012)**: elevators, escalators, stairs as "seed landmarks" with globally recognizable IMU signatures, plus "organic landmarks" (spots with unusual magnetic fluctuation or sensor signature) discovered per-building; dead-reckoning error is *periodically reset* at each detection, yielding 1.69 m median error with zero war-driving ([unloc.pdf](https://synrg.csl.illinois.edu/papers/unloc.pdf), [ACM](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/2594368.2602428)). **SemanticSLAM (IEEE TMC 2016)** formalizes this in a SLAM framework — landmark positions estimated to 0.83 m median, 33% faster convergence ([semantic_slam.pdf](https://synrg.csl.illinois.edu/papers/semantic_slam.pdf), [IEEE](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/7265092/)). **DynamicSLAM (2020)** adds *human encounters* as mobile anchors (not applicable solo, but interesting for grouped tours), reaching 1.1 m median, 55% better than prior art ([arXiv:2004.06621](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2004.06621)).

Reliability honesty check per landmark type:
- **Elevators**: the most reliable signature in the literature (distinct accelerometer pattern, near-perfect classification in UnLoc) — but mostly irrelevant if floor changes are out of scope.
- **Stairs/escalators**: reliably separable from walking via acceleration variance + magnetic variance ([UnLoc](https://synrg.csl.illinois.edu/papers/unloc.pdf)).
- **Doorways**: weakest. Raw-magnetometer door-crossing detection achieves only **~60% success and depends heavily on door material** (metal frames help, wooden doorways are nearly invisible) ([Zhao et al. 2015, summarized in Racko et al., arXiv:2209.11851](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2209.11851)). Magnetic-fingerprint door detection works better but needs per-door, per-direction fingerprints — which we effectively already get from the surveyor pass.
- **Turns/corners**: gyroscope turn detection is robust (it's a large, fast yaw event), and corner-matching against known route turns is one of the strongest cheap anchors — PDR systems using corner/door/stairs landmark correction report total error under 4% of distance traveled ([Springer landmark-PDR](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-981-13-7780-8_11)). For a *known ordered route*, a detected ~90° turn can only correspond to a handful of polyline vertices, making data association nearly trivial — this is the single best free anchor.

Design implication: treat landmark detections as **asymmetric evidence** — high-precision/low-recall. A confident turn or stair detection snaps the arc-length estimate; absence of detection should update nothing.

## 4. Other opportunistic phone signals (no install, no camera)

- **Ambient acoustics**: Batphone's Acoustic Background Spectrum gave 69% room identification across 33 rooms, 92% adjacent-room discrimination, with an open-source iPhone implementation ([Tarzia et al., MobiSys 2011](https://stevetarzia.com/papers/ABS_mobisys.pdf), [github.com/starzia/Batphone](https://github.com/starzia/Batphone)). There's recent follow-up ("Acoustic-assisted Indoor PDR," ISWC 2025, [ACM](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3715071.3750409)). Caveats: museums are quiet and acoustically homogeneous room-to-room compared to Batphone's test set; crowds shift the spectrum; and it needs a microphone session which conflicts with audio-guide playback (iOS allows simultaneous record+play with `.playAndRecord`, but the privacy optics of an always-on mic in a tourist app are bad). Viable as a *room-transition tiebreaker* at best.
- **Ambient light**: dead on iOS — no public ambient-light-sensor API; IOKit access is App Store-rejected; SensorKit requires special research entitlement ([Apple forums](https://developer.apple.com/forums/thread/36512), [SensorKit](https://developer.apple.com/documentation/sensorkit/srsensor/3377673-ambientlightsensor)). Without the camera (excluded by our constraints), skip entirely.
- **Wi-Fi**: the big iOS asymmetry. RSSI scanning of surrounding APs is **impossible** for third-party apps — there is no public scan API, NEHotspotHelper is restricted to actual hotspot-helper apps and caches stale results, and academic papers explicitly call iPhone Wi-Fi fingerprinting infeasible ([Apple forums](https://developer.apple.com/forums/thread/39204), [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11277-019-07015-4)). What *is* allowed: current connected SSID/BSSID via `NEHotspotNetwork.fetchCurrent` with the `com.apple.developer.networking.wifi-info` entitlement + precise-location permission ([Apple forums](https://developer.apple.com/forums/thread/670970)). If the venue has a guest network, *connection/disconnection* (and BSSID changes as the phone roams between APs!) is a coarse zone signal — BSSID roaming events are an underrated free breadcrumb in multi-AP venues. Apple's own Indoor Maps Program does Wi-Fi-fingerprint positioning with no hardware install, but requires venue enrollment with Apple and an Apple survey pass ([register.apple.com](https://register.apple.com/resources/indoor/program/indoor_maps), [WWDC19](https://developer.apple.com/videos/play/wwdc2019/245/)) — worth evaluating for flagship venues since it surfaces through plain Core Location.
- **Bluetooth ambient density**: CoreBluetooth foreground scanning without UUID filters is legal and returns advertisements from headphones, watches, exhibit electronics, etc. Background scanning requires declared service UUIDs and coalesces duplicates ([Punch Through guide](https://punchthrough.com/ios-ble-scanning-guide/), [Apple docs](https://developer.apple.com/library/archive/documentation/NetworkingInternetWeb/Conceptual/CoreBluetooth_concepts/CoreBluetoothBackgroundProcessingForIOSApps/PerformingTasksWhileYourAppIsInTheBackground.html)). Two problems: iOS hides MACs and rotates identifiers, and in a tour context most BLE devices are *other visitors* (mobile, non-stationary). Stationary venue electronics (fixed AV equipment advertising BLE) can act as accidental beacons if the surveyor records them — cheap to log during survey, use only what proves stable. Low effort, low-moderate payoff.
- **NFC tap points**: iPhone XS+ reads NDEF tags in the background (screen on) and routes Universal Links to the app — a €0.10 sticker per tour stop becomes a **zero-ambiguity, exact-position anchor** when tapped ([Apple Core NFC background reading](https://developer.apple.com/documentation/corenfc/adding-support-for-background-tag-reading), [GoToTags](https://gototags.com/help/ios/nfc/reading/background)). Technically this is "installing" something, but a sticker is not venue hardware in any meaningful sense. Opportunistic, user-initiated, perfect precision.
- **UWB / Find My companion**: NearbyInteraction gives ~10 cm ranging to U1-equipped third-party accessories ([WWDC21](https://developer.apple.com/videos/play/wwdc2021/10165/), [MartianCraft](https://martiancraft.com/blog/2023/04/nearby-interactions-part-one/)) — but that's hardware install; out of scope except as a future guided-group feature (range to the guide's phone).
- **Audio playback position as behavioral prior**: no direct academic literature found framing audio-guide playback as a localization observation, which makes it a genuinely novel, free signal for us. The closest work is location-aware adaptive museum guides (LISTEN, proactive-vs-reactive guide studies) showing tight coupling between content state and visitor position ([LISTEN](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/220116417_LISTEN_A_user-adaptive_audio-augmented_museum_guide), [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0953543811000567)). The mechanics are trivially Bayesian: P(position | playing stop-N audio) concentrates near stop N, ramping as playback progresses.

## 5. Behavioral / UX heuristics

Tour-product practice supports several implicit-confirmation patterns: VoiceMap's whole trigger model is effectively "position belief crossing a stop's geofence fires content," and conversely a *user-initiated* play is a position confession ([VoiceMap docs](https://docs.voicemap.me/tour-publishers/choosing-locations/)). Museum visitor-studies literature gives usable dwell priors: visitor attention/dwell distributions per exhibit are well characterized (Serrell's classic work on duration and allocation of visitor time, [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/229912168_Paying_Attention_The_Duration_and_Allocation_of_Visitors'_Time_in_Museum_Exhibitions)), and Bayesian hierarchical models of dwell time at exhibits exist ([PMC7334157](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7334157/)). For the filter: stationarity detection (step counter silent + low gyro energy) while stop-N audio plays = strong evidence the user is *at* stop N, which both anchors position and recalibrates per-user step length over the preceding leg — that step-length recalibration is the compounding win. Prompted micro-confirmations ("Are you facing the fountain?") should be a last-resort fallback fired only when filter variance exceeds a threshold; the proactive-vs-reactive guide literature warns unprompted interruptions damage the experience ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0953543811000567)).

## 6. Crowd-sourced / self-improving maps

The SLAM lineage says yes, ordinary user walks can refine the map: **FootSLAM** builds maps from odometry alone ([DLR/ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/236897464_FootSLAM_Pedestrian_Simultaneous_Localization_and_Mapping_Without_Exteroceptive_Sensors_-_Hitchhiking_on_Human_Perception_and_Cognition)); **MagSLAM** extends it with magnetic field measurements and achieved 9–22 cm accuracy, *exceeding* positioning against a pre-given magnetic map ([Semantic Scholar](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Simultaneous-Localization-and-Mapping-for-using-of-Robertson-Frassl/165caac7cf6e97e8f9a47a959480088b39c5ddec)); GraphSLAM-based crowdsourcing frameworks fuse free-walking users' Wi-Fi+PDR traces ([ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/315645273_GraphSLAM-based_Crowdsourcing_Framework_for_Indoor_Wi-Fi_Fingerprinting)); and crowdsourced magnetic trajectory clustering (DTW similarity + affinity propagation, then fusion) can build/refresh magnetic maps entirely from user data ([Sensors/PMC5712792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5712792/)). Our setting is *easier* than the general problem: users walk a known route in a known order, so each successful tour traverse is a labeled survey pass. The pragmatic v1: log magnetometer+PDR traces from real tours, accept traces where the filter stayed confident, time-warp them onto the route arc-length (DTW), and average into the fingerprint — turning a single surveyor pass into a continuously tightening ensemble that also captures device diversity and temporal drift of the magnetic environment. Commercial validation that this fusion stack works at scale: IndoorAtlas ships geomagnetic+PDR+Wi-Fi fusion claiming 2–5 m typical ([OpenHPS survey, arXiv:2101.05198](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2101.05198), [IndoorAtlas docs](https://docs.indooratlas.com/technical/release-notes/sdk-35-release-information/)).

## Ranked list: most cost-effective augmentations for this prototype

1. **Route-turn matching (gyro turn events ↔ polyline vertices)** — *both venue types.* Detected ±90°/180° turns snap the 1-D arc-length estimate to the nearest compatible route vertex. iOS: CoreMotion `CMDeviceMotion` yaw, already in the PDR. Cost: days. Benefit: largest drift reset per engineering hour; landmark-corrected PDR reaches <4% of distance error ([Springer](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-981-13-7780-8_11), [UnLoc](https://synrg.csl.illinois.edu/papers/unloc.pdf)).
2. **Audio-playback behavioral prior + dwell detection** — *both.* "Playing stop-N audio + stationary" = anchored at stop N; also recalibrates per-user step length each leg. iOS: we already own the player state; CoreMotion stationarity. Cost: days. Benefit: an anchor at every single tour stop, by construction — no other signal gives that. Apparently novel vs. literature ([LISTEN](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/220116417_LISTEN_A_user-adaptive_audio-augmented_museum_guide)).
3. **Accuracy-gated GPS as soft observation on the route** — *essential for semi-outdoor, useful in museums near windows/atriums/courtyards.* Weight fixes by `1/horizontalAccuracy²`, reject >50 m, fuse as 1-D constraint. iOS: plain `CLLocationManager`. Cost: days. Benefit: in gardens/castles this alone approaches VoiceMap-grade triggering (15–21 m radius proven in production, [VoiceMap](https://docs.voicemap.me/tour-publishers/choosing-locations/)); indoors it bounds gross error ([Zhou & Maekawa](https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3569467)).
4. **Entrance anchoring: last-good-outdoor-fix + coarse geofence prefetch** — *both.* Geofence (100 m) wakes the app and loads the route profile; the final sub-20 m outdoor fix initializes PDR origin and heading. iOS: region monitoring + standard updates ([Apple forums](https://developer.apple.com/forums/thread/94091)). Cost: days. Benefit: solves initialization, the worst failure mode of route-relative tracking.
5. **Stair/level-walkway signatures as ordered checkpoints** — *castles especially.* Stairs/ramps detected from accelerometer variance are high-precision, near-free anchors where the route includes them ([UnLoc](https://synrg.csl.illinois.edu/papers/unloc.pdf)). Cost: ~1 week. (Floors out of scope, but the *event* "user climbed the staircase between stops 4 and 5" is pure arc-length evidence.)
6. **Crowdsourced fingerprint refinement from confident tour traverses** — *both, compounding.* DTW-align accepted user traces onto route arc-length, ensemble-average into the magnetic profile ([PMC5712792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5712792/), [MagSLAM](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/165caac7cf6e97e8f9a47a959480088b39c5ddec)). Cost: 2–3 weeks incl. backend. Benefit: fixes the weak-field ambiguity problem over time and absorbs device variance.
7. **NFC tap stickers at key stops (optional per venue)** — *museums.* Background NDEF reading on iPhone XS+ deep-links into the app with exact position ([Apple](https://developer.apple.com/documentation/corenfc/adding-support-for-background-tag-reading)). Cost: days + sticker logistics. Benefit: perfect anchors where the venue permits; degrades gracefully (optional tap).
8. **Venue Wi-Fi SSID/BSSID roaming breadcrumbs** — *large museums with multi-AP guest Wi-Fi.* `NEHotspotNetwork.fetchCurrent` (entitlement + precise location) polled for BSSID changes as zone transitions ([Apple forums](https://developer.apple.com/forums/thread/670970)). Cost: ~1 week incl. survey of BSSID→zone mapping. Benefit: coarse but absolute zone signal where GPS is absent; useless in unconnected/semi-outdoor venues.
9. **Prompted micro-confirmations on filter-variance blowup** — *both, fallback.* "Tap when you reach the courtyard gate." Cost: days. Benefit: bounded worst case; use sparingly per HCI findings ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0953543811000567)).
10. **Ambient acoustic room fingerprints** — *museums, experimental.* 69% room ID / 92% adjacent-room discrimination ([Batphone](https://stevetarzia.com/papers/ABS_mobisys.pdf)); mic-privacy and crowd-noise concerns relegate it to a research spike, not roadmap.

**Skip entirely on iOS**: ambient light sensor (no API, [Apple forums](https://developer.apple.com/forums/thread/36512)), Wi-Fi RSSI fingerprinting (no scan API, [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11277-019-07015-4)), doorway magnetic detection as a primary anchor (~60% recall, material-dependent — [arXiv:2209.11851](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2209.11851)); fold doorway signatures into the magnetic profile instead of treating them as discrete events. Apple Indoor Maps Program enrollment is worth a parallel business-track investigation for flagship indoor venues ([Apple](https://register.apple.com/resources/indoor/program/indoor_maps)).

**Research limitations (from the agent):** the full text of Zhou & Maekawa was inaccessible (ACM paywall) — its quantitative error-reduction numbers are uncited here; the audio-playback-prior novelty claim rests on absence of search hits, not exhaustive review; CLVisit/geofence latency figures come from developer-community write-ups rather than Apple specifications; VoiceMap radii are publisher-documentation values, not independently measured.
