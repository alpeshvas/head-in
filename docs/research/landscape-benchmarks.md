# Indoor Positioning Landscape: Reality Check for Phone-Only, Route-Constrained Positioning

Date: 2026-06-10 · Produced by deep-research agent (web sources cited inline)

## 1. IndoorAtlas: the canonical magnetic-positioning company

**Tech.** Founded 2012 in Oulu, Finland by Prof. Janne Haverinen, spun out of robotics research after noticing that steel/concrete buildings distort the geomagnetic field consistently enough to form a per-building "fingerprint" ([Mobile Cloud Era analysis](https://mobilecloudera.com/indooratlas-geomagnetic-platform-disrupting-proximity-location-market/), [Wikipedia: Magnetic positioning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetic_positioning)). The production system is not magnetic-only: today's SDK fuses geomagnetic fingerprints with PDR, Wi-Fi (Android), barometer, and increasingly BLE beacons — their Dec 2024 release was a *beacon management* update, a telling drift back toward infrastructure ([IndoorAtlas](https://www.indooratlas.com/)).

**Claimed vs measured accuracy.** Marketing claims 1–2 m. The most-cited "independent" validation is a 2014 Opus Research walkthrough in one US retail store reporting "within several feet" on an iPhone 5 ([InfoStride/Opus](https://www.theinfostride.com/opus-research-finds-indooratlas-technology-demonstrates-consistent-accuracy-within-several-feet-in-a-major-retail-environment/)) — a sponsored-adjacent analyst demo, not a benchmark. Academic literature on pure magnetic positioning consistently shows it needs motion history (sequence matching) and degrades with device heading, holding posture, and temporal field changes; magnetic-only systems rarely beat 2–5 m without fusion ([Sensors 2022 analysis](https://doi.org/10.3390/s22114014), [Sensors 2023 improvement study](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10457947/)). No credible independent study reproduces sustained 1 m phone-only accuracy in arbitrary venues.

**Business trajectory.** Raised ~$27.5M total, including $10M from Baidu (2014, exclusive China deal) and a Yahoo! Japan-led Series B (2017) ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/indooratlas-receives-us-10-million-010100374.html), [Global Venturing](https://globalventuring.com/indooratlas-pinpoints-yahoo-japan-for-series-b/)). Thirteen years later: **~$1.3M revenue, 12 employees** ([GetLatka](https://getlatka.com/companies/indooratlas.com), [Tracxn](https://tracxn.com/d/companies/indooratlas/__hYVqtl3E39nMu9xwdF8OsqW0P7jy-NOK4_acJM8c-GM)). Not dead — still shipping SDK 3.7/3.8 in 2025 and doing event-app deals ([Owler](https://www.owler.com/company/indooratlas)) — but $27.5M-in → $1.3M ARR is the post-mortem: the technology works *well enough to demo, not well enough to disappear*. No published founder post-mortem exists, but the observable pattern is (a) per-venue magnetic surveying and re-surveying is a real operational cost; (b) accuracy is environment-dependent so sales cycles required pilots; (c) the "killer app" (retail proximity marketing) never monetized; (d) they ended up adding beacons anyway, erasing their differentiator.

## 2. Apple

**Indoor Maps Program / IMDF: alive but narrow.** The program still exists at [register.apple.com/indoor](https://register.apple.com/indoor), is free, and IMDF is an [OGC Community Standard](https://www.ogc.org/standards/indoor-mapping-data-format/). But Apple's own FAQ now states indoor maps in Apple Maps are limited to "publicly accessible areas of **airports**" (plus legacy malls), and the program's docs haven't meaningfully changed since the [WWDC19 announcement](https://developer.apple.com/videos/play/wwdc2019/245/) ([FAQ](https://register.apple.com/resources/indoor/program/faq)). It's in maintenance mode, not deprecated.

**What Core Location gives you.** At *enrolled* venues with 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi coverage, Core Location returns RF-fingerprint+PDR positions with `CLFloor` floor numbers — described as "GPS-level accuracy" indoors (i.e., ~5–10 m, not 1 m) — and **third-party apps do benefit automatically** through standard `CLLocationManager` at enrolled venues ([Apple FAQ](https://register.apple.com/resources/indoor/program/faq), [Mappedin guide](https://www.mappedin.com/resources/blog/indoor-positioning-made-easy-with-apples-core-location/), [Esri/ArcGIS Indoors](https://www.esri.com/arcgis-blog/products/arcgis-indoors/indoor-gis/1948632-2)). The catch: a museum must enroll, survey with Apple's tools, and have dense Wi-Fi; coverage of museums is effectively nil. Critically, **iOS gives third-party apps no raw Wi-Fi scan data** (privacy), which kills the single most informative fingerprinting signal used by every Android-based competition winner ([Navigine 2025](https://navigine.com/blog/wifi-for-indoor-positioning-and-navigation/), [Crowd Connected 2026 guide](https://www.crowdconnected.com/blog/indoor-positioning-tech-update-2026/)). Apple bought WiFiSLAM for $20M in 2013 — explicitly citing museum audio tours as a use case — and kept the capability first-party ([AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/13/03/24/apple-buys-indoor-gps-company-wifislam-for-20m)).

**New APIs since 2024:** nothing indoor-specific. WWDC23/24 brought `CLLocationUpdate`, `CLMonitor`, `CLServiceSession` (ergonomics/authorization, not new indoor signals) ([WWDC24](https://developer.apple.com/videos/play/wwdc2024/10212/)). Wi-Fi RTT (802.11mc/az) remains unsupported on iOS ([Crowd Connected](https://www.crowdconnected.com/blog/indoor-positioning-tech-update-2026/)). Available signals are what we're already using: magnetometer, IMU/CMPedometer, barometer, plus opportunistic GPS near windows/entrances.

## 3. Benchmarks: what phone-only actually achieves

The IPIN/EvAAL competition is the closest thing to ground truth. Ranking metric is **third-quartile (75th percentile) point error** over a realistic multi-floor walk by an independent actor ([EvAAL framework](https://evaal.aaloa.org/)):

- **IPIN 2023, Track 1 (smartphone, onsite, real-time):** winner THWS Würzburg **2.8 m**; 2nd place 6.2 m; 3rd 9.1 m ([EvAAL 2023 results](https://evaal.aaloa.org/2023/competition-results)).
- **IPIN 2024, Track 1:** winner THWS & cronn **5.5 m**; 2nd 6.4 m; 3rd >30 m ([IPIN 2024 results](https://competition.ipin-conference.org/2024/results)).
- **IPIN 2023, Track 3 (smartphone, offsite):** winner 5.3 m; **IPIN 2021 Track 3:** winner GD-WHU (Gaode/AMAP + Wuhan Univ.) 4.43 m ([IPIN 2021 results](https://ipin-conference.org/2021/competitionresults.html)).
- Best smartphone result ever in the series: ~1 m third-quartile at IPIN 2020 (offline, with full Wi-Fi access on Android) ([IPIN 2020 paper](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/351826977_Off-line_Evaluation_of_Indoor_Positioning_Systems_in_Different_Scenarios_The_Experiences_from_IPIN_2020_Competition)).

Winners universally fuse **Wi-Fi RSS/FTM + PDR + magnetic + floor-plan map-matching in a particle filter** ([IPIN 2019 paper](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/345939445_The_IPIN_2019_Indoor_Localisation_Competition_-Description_and_Results), [example winning-style system](https://ceur-ws.org/Vol-2498/short42.pdf)). Note: these are *Android* numbers with Wi-Fi scanning; iOS-feasible signal sets (magnetic+PDR+map only) sit at the worse end.

The **Microsoft Indoor Localization Competition** (IPSN 2014–2018, >100 teams) found infrastructure-free systems averaged **1.5–5.3 m**, comparable to many infrastructure-based ones; the famous sub-meter results (e.g., CMU's 0.47 m in 2018) required deployed ultrasound/UWB hardware ([lessons-learned paper](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/313746557_Microsoft_indoor_localization_competition_Experiences_and_lessons_learned), [IPSN 2015 official results](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/event/microsoft-indoor-localization-competition-ipsn-2015/official-results/), [CMU](https://engineering.cmu.edu/news-events/news/2018/07/05-indoor-localization.html)). A decade of competitive pressure has not moved phone-only below ~2–3 m P75 in unfamiliar buildings. That's the physics-and-survey ceiling, not a lack of cleverness.

**Our advantage:** route constraint. Competition tracks are free walks over whole buildings. A known 1-D route with checkpoint sequence matching converts a 2-D problem into along-track localization — academic sequence-based magnetic matching on constrained paths (DTW/particle filter) regularly reports 1–3 m along-track ([magnetic fingerprint studies](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8434502/), [VSLAM+magnetic fusion](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC9738752/)).

## 4. Other infrastructure-free commercial players

- **Mapsted** (Canada): claims 1–5 m (sometimes "1 m") hardware-free via magnetic+IMU+barometer fusion, 110+ patents; evidence is overwhelmingly self-published marketing; some real deployments (Strong Museum of Play, University of Windsor) via [Gartner Peer Insights](https://www.gartner.com/reviews/market/indoor-location-services/vendor/mapsted)/[G2](https://www.g2.com/products/mapsted/reviews) ([Mapsted](https://mapsted.com/indoor-positioning-system)). No independent benchmark results; they have never entered IPIN. Credibility: medium-low on accuracy claims, real as a business.
- **Oriient** (Israel): geomagnetic-only "IndoorGPS," claims ~1 m, $11M Series A 2022, deployed in 1,500+ grocery stores (Albertsons, Carrefour) ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2022/05/11/oriient-raises-11m-series-a-for-its-indoor-positioning-service/), [Oriient](https://www.oriient.me/geomagnetic-indoor-positioning-technology/)). Most credible magnetic player today, but note the domain: grocery aisles are a *route-constrained, high-magnetic-texture* environment — effectively the same trick we're attempting. 1 m claims again lack third-party verification.
- **Newer (2024–2026):** ZaiNar (RF time-of-arrival via existing 5G/Wi-Fi infra, unicorn valuation, 90+ patents — infrastructure-light, not phone-only) ([Entrepreneur Loop](https://entrepreneurloop.com/zainar-emerges-from-stealth-how-a-gps-alternative-startup-just-achieved-unicorn-status/), [The Information](https://www.theinformation.com/newsletters/ai-agenda/startup-developing-gps-alternative-physical-ai-earns-1-billion-valuation)); Juxta (YC S25, "GPS alternative, no hardware," pre-product) ([YC](https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/juxta)). No new entrant claims solved phone-only indoor positioning; the 2026 practitioner consensus is that on iPhone "no phone-native solution is currently viable" without beacons ([Crowd Connected 2026](https://www.crowdconnected.com/blog/indoor-positioning-tech-update-2026/) — a BLE vendor, so discount accordingly).

## 5. How tour products actually handle positioning

- **Bloomberg Connects** (Met, MoMA, 700+ venues): **manual lookup numbers** typed by the visitor ([Met](https://www.metmuseum.org/bloomberg-connects), [Bloomberg Connects FAQ](https://www.bloombergconnects.org/faq/)). The best-funded player in the space chose zero positioning.
- **Nubart**: QR-code-triggered web audio guides, explicitly anti-app; they publish data that only **2.47% of visitors download museum apps** ([Nubart](https://www.nubart.eu/audio-guides/museum-audio-guide-app-adoption-rates.html)) — a sobering funnel number for any app-based product.
- **VoiceMap**: GPS auto-play outdoors; indoors falls back to a **manual "I am here" button** with photos and directions; GPS used only for the start point ([VoiceMap indoor docs](https://docs.voicemap.me/tour-publishers/indoor-tours/)).
- Industry guidance for audio guides ranks QR/NFC/numbered stops above beacons on cost/reliability ([Musa guide](https://www.musa.guide/en/resources/beacons-nfc-qr-codes-audio-guides)).
- **Nobody in the tour market ships passive sensor positioning.** That's both the opportunity (genuine differentiation: hands-in-pocket auto-advancing tours) and a warning (multiple well-capitalized teams looked at this and chose buttons).

## 6. Patents / IP

Magnetic indoor positioning is a dense patent thicket but litigation-quiet — no known infringement suits in the space. Key holders:

- **IndoorAtlas Oy**: a substantial portfolio on magnetic map generation and matching, e.g. [US9316501 "Generating magnetic field map for indoor positioning"](https://patents.google.com/patent/US9316501B2/un), US9599473 (magnetic-field navigation), US11428533 (generating magnetic fingerprints), US9683851 — full list at [Justia](https://patents.justia.com/assignee/indooratlas-oy). Broad claims cover crowdsourced/walk-survey magnetic map construction and particle-filter matching.
- **Mapsted**: 110+ patents on hardware-free sensor-fusion positioning ([Mapsted](https://mapsted.com/)).
- Qualcomm, Apple, InvenSense/TDK (Trusted Positioning) hold adjacent IMU+map-matching patents.

Practical read: for a prototype and even a niche tour product, risk is low (small patentees don't troll, and many core patents are pre-2014 with expiry approaching ~2032–2034; the foundational academic work — Haverinen & Kemppainen 2009 — is prior art constraining claim breadth). If this becomes a commercial SDK competing with IndoorAtlas/Mapsted, get an FTO review of the IndoorAtlas Justia list specifically around *magnetic map generation from survey walks* and *sequence-based magnetic matching*. ## (a) Realistic accuracy expectations — magnetic fingerprinting + PDR, iPhone, surveyed routes

| Scenario | Realistic P50 | Realistic P75 | Notes / evidence base |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free 2-D positioning, whole building, iOS (no Wi-Fi scans) | 4–8 m | 8–15 m | iOS lacks Wi-Fi RSS; competition winners *with* Wi-Fi got 2.8–5.5 m P75 ([IPIN 2023](https://evaal.aaloa.org/2023/competition-results), [IPIN 2024](https://competition.ipin-conference.org/2024/results)) |
| **Along-track on a known surveyed route (our case)**, user walking continuously, phone in hand | **1–3 m** | **3–6 m** | Route constraint + magnetic sequence matching (DTW/particle filter); consistent with [magnetic sequence literature](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8434502/) and Oriient's aisle-constrained deployments |
| Same, phone in pocket / arbitrary posture | 3–6 m | 6–12 m | Magnetometer orientation + PDR step-model degradation ([Sensors 2022](https://doi.org/10.3390/s22114014)) |
| Checkpoint *arrival detection* (binary, checkpoints ≥8–10 m apart) | ~90–95% correct trigger within ±5 m | — | This reframing is where the approach is genuinely strong |
| Stationary user (lingering at an exhibit) | drifts/ambiguous | — | PDR gives nothing when still; magnetic point-match is ambiguous; design dwell states explicitly |
| Across iPhone models / OS updates / building changes (HVAC, exhibits moved) | expect periodic re-survey | — | Steel exhibit cases and moved infrastructure shift local field; IndoorAtlas's operational pain |

**Design implication:** don't promise a blue dot. Promise reliable *checkpoint triggering with ordered-route priors*, which the P75 supports if checkpoints are spaced ≥2× P75 error.

## (b) Three biggest lessons from prior attempts

1. **Survey/maintenance burden, not algorithm accuracy, is what killed the category commercially.** IndoorAtlas raised $27.5M and landed at $1.3M ARR with 12 people ([GetLatka](https://getlatka.com/companies/indooratlas.com)); they and everyone else discovered every venue needs surveying, re-surveying after physical changes, and per-venue QA — a services business wearing an SDK costume. The route-constrained model slashes this (survey one path, not a building), but build re-survey tooling and drift detection from day one; it's the product, not a chore.
2. **Phone-only has a hard ~2–3 m floor and iOS is the worst platform for it.** Ten years of IPIN/Microsoft competitions never broke ~2.8 m P75 onsite even with Android Wi-Fi access ([IPIN 2023](https://evaal.aaloa.org/2023/competition-results), [Microsoft lessons](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/313746557_Microsoft_indoor_localization_competition_Experiences_and_lessons_learned)); iOS denies Wi-Fi scans entirely ([Navigine](https://navigine.com/blog/wifi-for-indoor-positioning-and-navigation/)). Winners that survived (Oriient) picked use cases where 2–3 m *along a constrained path* is enough. Any product plan requiring "which painting am I in front of" at <1.5 m on iPhone will fail; "which room/stop am I at" will work.
3. **The market repeatedly chose dumb-but-reliable over smart-but-flaky — and the app-download funnel is brutal.** Bloomberg Connects (manual numbers), VoiceMap indoors ("I am here" button), Nubart (QR, plus their 2.47% app-adoption stat) all ship manual positioning ([VoiceMap docs](https://docs.voicemap.me/tour-publishers/indoor-tours/), [Nubart](https://www.nubart.eu/audio-guides/museum-audio-guide-app-adoption-rates.html)). Passive positioning must *degrade gracefully into* manual selection (auto-suggest the stop, one-tap confirm) rather than replace it; a wrong auto-trigger is worse for trust than no auto-trigger.

## (c) Build / buy / partner recommendation

**Build — but build the narrow thing.** Buying is unattractive: IndoorAtlas/Mapsted/Oriient sell building-wide 2-D positioning with per-venue pricing and surveying anyway — we'd inherit their cost structure without their scale, and Oriient is retail-focused. Partnering with Apple is a dead end (airports-only, venue-side enrollment, no developer-facing signal access — [Apple FAQ](https://register.apple.com/resources/indoor/program/faq)). Meanwhile no incumbent serves *route-constrained tour triggering*, and the academic recipe for it (PDR + magnetic sequence matching + 1-D particle filter on a route graph) is well-published and within a small team's reach. Concretely: (1) frame the product as checkpoint detection, not continuous positioning; (2) gate go/no-go on a measured metric — ≥90% correct checkpoint triggers within ±5 m across ≥3 venues, 3+ iPhone models, hand and pocket carry; (3) keep one-tap manual confirm as the permanent fallback UX; (4) consider offering optional "2 BLE beacons at ambiguous junctions" as a pragmatic escape hatch — every pure-software player eventually added it; (5) do a cheap FTO pass over the [IndoorAtlas patent list](https://patents.justia.com/assignee/indooratlas-oy) before commercial launch.
