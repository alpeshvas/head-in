## Survey UX: ad-hoc checkpoints (added 2026-06-19)

Leave the Setup checkpoint list EMPTY to enter ad-hoc mode: during recording, type the upcoming checkpoint's name (optional) while walking up to it, then tap "Drop checkpoint" at the exact arrival instant (typing never delays the anchor timestamp; empty → auto "Checkpoint N", renameable offline). Predefined-list mode is unchanged. The pipeline already reconstructs everything from `anchor` lines, so no analysis-side change was needed; ad-hoc sessions also write the final ordered name list into the `end` line (meta.checkpoints is empty for them). `RecordingController.isAdHoc` = empty setup list; `dropCheckpoint(pendingName:)` / `recordedCheckpoints`. After an ad-hoc pass, the dropped names auto-populate the Setup checkpoint field, so pass 2+ of the same route uses the fast predefined tap-through with matching names (build-profile merges segments by name) — ad-hoc bootstraps a route, then it's an ordinary multi-pass survey.

## Filter architecture (both implementations)

240-bins-per-segment grid over concatenated segment profiles + OFF state. Step-driven: predictStep (per-segment stride, σ=0.35·stride, backward tail, OFF leak 0.02, start AND end bins are absorbing barriers), predictIdle when standing. Emission per step: last 6 step-intervals resampled per-step to bin units, **stride-lag first-difference Gaussian** with single fitted noise (diffSigmaUT 2.42), tempered (indep bins=8), flat gate 3 µT, frozen when magnetometer uncalibrated and in the 2-stride terminal region. Turn anchors: matched (sign+magnitude tolerance 55° AND ≥10% posterior support within 3σ) → positional bump ×OFF 0.3; unmatched ≥100° → 0.5 OFF injection + 8-step reversal leak. Checkpoint: P(s≥anchor−stride/2)>0.8 twice consecutively + observation within last 2 steps + NOT reversal-active + P(OFF)<0.5. Off-route: P(OFF)>0.5 sustained 3 s. No ratchets except the UI (timeline fire-once-forward + displayBin floor, deliberate).

## Known limitations

- **Pacing false-advances on a route whose own turn is a U-turn, in a weak-field venue** (Ravi-place, 2026-06-19, `..._live_20260619-102451`). Pacing in the hall marched belief forward 29 steps to bin 422; a pacing U-turn (+198°) then MATCHED the route's +170° signature turn (sigmaBins 35), snapped belief past Balcony+Kitvhen, and collapsed P(OFF) 0.62→0 — firing both. Root: the route's only turn IS a ~U-turn, so pacing U-turns match it (the support gate passes once forward-marched belief reaches the turn bin's 3σ window). Tried fix: reject turn match when P(OFF) ≥ 0.4 (a confident-on-route gate) — REVERTED: it both failed to fully stop Ravi pacing AND regressed Ravi's own forward walk (4/4→3, flagged off-route), because Ravi's weak field drives P(OFF) high even during legitimate walks, so no P(OFF) threshold separates pacing-U-turn from route-U-turn. Conclusion: Ravi-place (small home, U-turn loop, weak field) is near the limit of the in-order route-constrained approach; tuning to defend pacing breaks the legit walk. Best paths: re-survey a non-doubling-back route (avoid the ~180° turn so pacing U-turns find nothing to match, as Test/Plumeria pacing is defended), or the free-roam per-zone architecture, or per-room absolute cues. Documented, not fixed.
- **Route-order assumption — out-of-order / shortcut / reverse walks fire in route order, not physical order** (observed 2026-06-19, Ravi-place `..._live_20260619-100523`). User went Checkpoint1 → Kitchen → Balcony (out of order); app fired Balcony then Kitvhen (route order) by step-driven forward progress; pacing after correctly fired nothing; P(OFF) rose to 0.50 but didn't sustain. NOT a crash (the truncated no-`end` trace was a force-quit; parity passes on this profile). Root cause = the design itself: a 1-D "arc length along ONE route walked in order" filter cannot represent "which room am I in" or non-monotonic paths. Supporting arbitrary movement needs a different architecture (independent per-zone fingerprint matching / 2-D model, or per-room absolute cues BLE/NFC) — a fork from the route-constrained design, not a tuning fix. The reverse-walk case below is a special case of this.
- **No start/direction guard — a reverse or mid-route entry false-advances** (observed 2026-06-19, LIS trace `..._live_20260619-031803`). Walking the forward profile backward (C8→C1) still fired C1–C6: the filter initializes at bin 0 and `predictStep` only advances forward, so it marches on step count with P(OFF)≈0 while the magnitude-symmetric emission fails to veto the reversed traversal. Consistent with the directional-route design (forward/reverse are separate profiles) but the filter doesn't *reject* misuse, it silently tracks forward. Research-aligned fix: **entrance/start anchoring** (Phase-3 GPS/entrance init) — don't arm checkpoint firing until the start region is confidently matched.
  - **Magnetic-only entrance anchoring ATTEMPTED 2026-06-19 and REVERTED.** Built: arm checkpoint firing only after ≥2 early emission argmaxes land in the start region (+ rising-edge fire gate + pin-belief-while-unarmed). The discriminator is real (reverse walk's argmax sits at the far end where C8 physically is, forward's at the start) and fully suppressed the LIS reverse walk — but the argmax-vote arming did **not generalize**: it never triggered on the short Test (~12 m) and office-right-wing routes, so pinning held belief at the start and they fired **0 checkpoints** (regression). Pointwise magnetic magnitude is too weakly discriminative to robustly confirm "at the start" across routes — exactly why the research pairs entrance-init with **GPS/absolute cue**. Reverted to baseline (all routes fire again).
  - **Compass/heading direction check TESTED 2026-06-19 and rejected.** Idea: use compass heading (directional) where magnitude is direction-blind. Computed phone-heading-minus-field-bearing over forward vs reverse LIS walks → **~115° both, 1° apart (not the ~180° flip needed)**. Reason: that quantity reduces to the angle between the phone body and the local field — it depends on how the phone is HELD, not the direction of TRAVEL. Hand-held phone attitude is decoupled from travel direction (the RoNIN heading-agnostic premise). So absolute compass cannot reject a reverse walk.
  - **Web research 2026-06-19 → [docs/research/direction-and-entrance-anchoring.md](research/direction-and-entrance-anchoring.md).** Key correction: a hand-held phone's COMPASS can't give travel direction, but GAIT analysis of acceleration can (PCA-GA: forward axis = max horizontal-accel variance, 180° resolved from gait-cycle asymmetry; P50/P75 heading error 5.6°/9.2°, no training). Literature confirms magnetic magnitude is direction-symmetric (forward = inverse of backward pattern). RECOMMENDED fix: gait travel-heading vs the route's heading profile (from survey ARKit GT) — ~180° mismatch ⇒ reverse ⇒ don't fire. Lighter than RoNIN, works on low-turn routes where turn-matching is weak.
  - Robust fix paths, in order: (a) **gait-based travel heading + route-heading check** (recommended, no new hardware/training — see research doc); (b) **product-level** — the user pressing Start at the entrance IS the entrance anchor (the reverse walk was deliberate misuse; guided tours enter at start); (c) **turn-SEQUENCE direction check** — rotation rate IS travel-coupled; a reverse traversal yields the route's turns in reversed/sign-flipped order. Weak on low-turn routes, noisy in cluttered offices (reverse LIS turns: 1/6 matched), so not reliable alone; (c) **absolute cue** — NFC/QR tap at the entrance, or accuracy-gated GPS at semi-outdoor venues (original research plan, blocked on venue); (d) **travel direction from RoNIN-class odometry** (Phase 4) or global magnetic localization via uniform init (bigger rewrite, Magicol ~20 s-to-localize risk). Accepted as a documented limitation for now.
